The Pokémon Abode - Early Years

In 8th grade, I started a website about a videogame I loved - Pokémon. It flourished during the first dot-com-boom, partnering with IGN.com for sponsorship. As revenue rolled in, the site expanded with a webforum for thousands of members, sub-sites and staff. The site became one of the largest Pokémon sites on the internet.

The Pokémon Abode - The End

I was invited to the E3 videogame conference, and attended a Pokémon summit in where I spoke to the creators of the Pokémon franchise. But entering its 3rd year, revenue stopped and I had to close the site - it became a victim of the dot-com-bomb. However, the experience shaped a creative and entreprenurial spirit.

Eagle Scout

Throughout my early years, I was in the Boy Scouts and rose through the program, performing community service, troop events, backpacking expeditions and leadership training. The capstone of the experience was earning the rank of Eagle Scout, with my final service project repairing park structures pictured above.

UCSD Nikkei Student Union

In undergrad I immediately got involved in the Japanese American student community. Starting in general staff, I helped with mentorship, community service and cultural events, and served as webmaster, making lifelong friends along the way. In my 4th year joined the Executive Board, handling communications, outreach, and leadership.

YMCA Reach and Rise Mentor Program

After moving back to San Francisco, I went back into mentoring by joining the Chinatown YMCA's Reach and Rise program, mentoring at-risk youth. We are paired with a single mentee for one year, during which time we provide guidance, support and act as a friend for those without a traditional familial or academic support structure.

Volunteering with UCLA Alumni Association

I attended UCLA for graduate school. With a very strong network in the Bay Area, UCLA's Alumni Association provided a community with social and volunteer events that I participate in. Pictured here, we are running a UCLA information booth at San Francisco's Pride 2015 Parade, shortly after the Supreme Court legalized same-sex marriage.

Traveling

Exploring new cultures, landscapes and experiences is a constant passion. I strive to see (and photograph) what I haven't seen, and to experience and appreciate the diversity of the world. To this point, I have been fortunate enough to visit 18 countries, with 1 more on deck for 2015.

Traveling - Syrian Refugee Crisis

By chance in August 2015 I ended up on the last train from Budapest to Vienna that allowed Syrian refugees, before they denied further movement - I got a glimpse of their unfortunate journey and struggle to escape the civil war. Seeing the outpour of support was inspiring, but the bureaucratic process preventing them was frustrating.

Passing the Time: Running

I picked up long distance running as a therapeutic tool several years ago, and have turned it into a full-time passion. I run 2-3 race events per year, up to half-marathons. I recently reached a personal half-marathon time goal in San Francisco, and plan to run one in Brooklyn in the Spring of 2016.

Passing the Time: Snowboarding

Growing up in driving distance Lake Tahoe, skiing and then snowboarding became one of my biggest hobbies. With the advent of action cameras, I've documented each season (and learned video editing), which has taken me across the West Coast and Canada. 2013's season is above, while the rest are on YouTube.

Passing the Time: Motorsports

I've always been a gearhead, fixing and modifying cars since before I could drive thanks to a very knowledgable father, who also taught me to ride motorcycles. I've been riding for 10+ years, enjoying the California scenery and the feeling of adrenaline as well as control.

Photography

One of my proudest pastimes is photography, which I picked up during undergrad. Coupled with traveling, I love to capture beautiful landscapes, culturally unique sights and artistic installations. Some of my favorite shots are in my Photo Portfolio.

CORNELL TECH MBA

Cornell Tech's central location in Manhattan will help to further develop my creative and personal characteristics during my time there.

UCSD Holograms

I studied optics and photonics in undergrad, and we created a projectable hologram that pops out of a piece of glass from a red laser pointer. The "recording" process was only 1 second, but the image lasts forever and is a fascinating concept, as this photo was taken 8 years later.
