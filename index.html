The Pokémon Abode - Early Years

In 8th grade, I started a website about a videogame I loved - Pokémon. It flourished during the first dot-com-boom, partnering with IGN.com for sponsorship. As revenue rolled in, the site expanded with a webforum for thousands of members, sub-sites and staff. The site became one of the largest Pokémon sites on the internet. More info

The Pokémon Abode - The End

I was invited to the E3 videogame conference, and attended Nintendo's Pokémon summit where I spoke to the creators of the Pokémon franchise. But entering its 3rd year, revenue stopped and I had to close the site - it became a victim of the dot-com-bomb. However, the experience unlocked a creative and entreprenurial spirit.

Eagle Scout

Throughout my early years, I was in the Boy Scouts and rose through the program, performing community service, troop events, backpacking expeditions and leadership training. The capstone of the experience was earning the rank of Eagle Scout, with my final service project repairing park structures pictured above.

UCSD Nikkei Student Union

In undergrad I immediately got involved in the Japanese American student community. Starting in general staff, I helped with mentorship, community service and cultural events, making lifelong friends along the way. I also served as webmaster, and in my 4th year joined the Executive Board, handling communications, outreach, and leadership.

YMCA Reach and Rise Mentor Program

After moving back to San Francisco, I went back into mentoring by joining the Chinatown YMCA's Reach and Rise program, mentoring at-risk youth. We are paired with a single mentee for one year, during which time we provide guidance, support and act as a friend for those without a traditional familial or academic support structure.

Volunteering with UCLA Alumni Association

I attended UCLA for graduate school. With a very strong network in the Bay Area, UCLA's Alumni Association provided a community with social and volunteer events that I participate in. Pictured here, we are running a UCLA information booth at San Francisco's Pride 2015 Parade, shortly after the Supreme Court legalized same-sex marriage.

Traveling

Exploring new cultures, landscapes and experiences is a constant passion. I strive to see (and photograph) what I haven't seen, and to experience and appreciate the diversity of the world. To this point, I have been fortunate enough to visit 18 countries, with 1 more on deck for 2015.

Traveling - Syrian Refugee Crisis

By chance in August 2015 I ended up on the last train from Budapest to Vienna that allowed Syrian refugees, before they denied further movement - I got a glimpse of their unfortunate journey and struggle to escape the civil war. Seeing the outpour of support was inspiring, but the bureaucratic process preventing them was frustrating.

UCSD Holography Lab

I studied optics and photonics in undergrad, and we created a projectable hologram that pops out of a piece of glass from a red laser pointer. The "recording" process was only 1 second, but the image lasts forever and is a fascinating concept, as this photo was taken 8 years later.

UCLA MEMS Lab

Studying solid-state electronics in graduate school, I often worked in the microelectromechanical systems (MEMS) and nanomaterials lab creating structures and systems to demonstrate the next generation in materials technology.

UCLA Nanolab

Much of my graduate work concentrated on gallium-arsenide (GaAs) nanostructures; in the image above, a scanning electron microscope was used to etch my name into a piece of GaAs with walls only tens-of-nanometers thick.

UCLA Master's Research - Resistivity in GaAs Nanowires

My Master's project was a Matlab-based model to determine the effect of surface-level electric charges on the resistivity of nanowires - at the nano scale, electrical properties are vastly different. More info is available in my Master's project slides.

Raytheon Engineering Rotation Program

I was part of a group of 10 engineers, selected for versatility, ambition and leadership potential. This group was afforded the opportunity to rotate within Raytheon's various business and technical centers. The program provided a foundation for leadership and a community of peers who continuously learned from each other's experiences.

Raytheon VIIRS

My final engineering project at Raytheon was working on NASA's Joint Polar Satellite System, on the Visible Infrared Imaging Radiometer Suite (VIIRS) - an imaging satellite monitoring weather and the earth. I lead technician teams in assembly of the optical components of the system.

RD Design - Web Development

As a side business starting in 2013, I re-approached web design and development and started making websites for small business clients to keep my coding abilities fresh. The sites ranged from flower shops to Kickstarter campaigns, and flexed my design sense as well as my learning and adaptability for the constantly changing landscape of front-end engineering.

IONSYS®

I joined The Medicines Company (MDCO), a pharmaceutical company, in the end of 2013. Working on IONSYS®, a post-surgical pain medication delivery device, I combined my engineering background with new experiences in regulatory documentation, quality and project management. The small size of our group forced us to wear many hats, and adapt quickly.

MDCO - International Audits

Serving as a technical consult during quality process control audits at suppliers in Shenzhen (left) and Guangzhou (right), I gained a great deal of experience working with international teams - right down to dealing with language and cultural gaps between interacting companies. Future audits to the Philippines and Italy are planned.

MDCO - IONSYS® App

Originally winning a company challenge to create an innovative idea, my proposal to build an iPad app for sales and education drew much excitement. Despite subsequently cancelled funding, I pushed on independently to collaborate with our marketing and developer teams to design and create the product - my first taste into product management.

MDCO - IONSYS Launch / Approval

After more than a year of documentation, FDA audits, cross-team collaboration, engineering design and testing, and a great deal of learning, our IONSYS® device was approved by the FDA in April 2015. Currently the device, and my app to supplement its sale and training, is making its way to hospitals across the country.

Post-MBA Goal: Product Management

The process of building the IONSYS® App made me leverage my technical knowledge while also coordinating our marketing and development teams, to understand the market needs and strategies and build a successful product, which I loved. Playing this role of influence as a product manager is my post-MBA goal.
